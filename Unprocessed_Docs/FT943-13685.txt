
The four east European states making the most progress with their economic
reforms should be invited to future European Union summits, Chancellor
Helmut Kohl (pictured above right) yesterday told Hungary's visiting prime
minister, Mr Gyula Horn (above left), writes Michael Lindemann in Bonn.
Hungary, Poland, the Czech Republic and Slovakia should attend at least one
summit a year, he said. Mr Horn was on his first official visit since being
confirmed as premier last week.
