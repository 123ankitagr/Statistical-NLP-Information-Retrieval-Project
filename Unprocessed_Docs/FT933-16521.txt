
How did Britain's public finances get out of control? What should the
government do now?  How should Kenneth Clarke shape his November budget?
Bill Robinson (left), former director of the Institute for Fiscal Studies
and special adviser to chancellor Norman Lamont from February 1991 until the
cabinet reshuffle in May, will offer some answers in an FT eight-part summer
series. In his first article, on Tuesday, Robinson asks: how do current
problems compare with the crisis of 1976? There are, he concludes, alarming
similarities.
