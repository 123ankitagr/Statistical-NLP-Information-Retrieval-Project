
The 1960 United States national hockey team, which upset the Soviet Union and Czechoslovakia to win the gold medal at Squaw Valley, has been named to the U.S. Olympic Hall of Fame. 
Also named were Mel Sheppard, last American to win the 1,500-meter run; Lee Evans, whose 400-meter record stood for 20 years; Bobby Joe Morrow, who won gold medals in the 100- and 200-meter sprints in 1956, the first to do so since Jesse Owens; boxer Joe Frazier, who earned a gold in 1964; weightlifter John Davis, who won golds in 1948 and 1952, and Roone Arledge, president of ABC News.
