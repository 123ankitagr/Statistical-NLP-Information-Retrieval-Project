
Hungary's Kristina Egerszegi became the first individual double gold
medalist at the Games with an Olympic record, adding the top medal in the
100m backstroke to her triumph in the 400m individual medley.
Alexandre Popov of the Unified Team won the men's 100m freestyle.
Spain's Martin Lopez-Zubero won the men's 200m backstroke and, like
Egerszegi, set an Olympic record.
The American women's relay quartet won in 3 minutes, 39.46 seconds, breaking
the world record of 3:40.57 set by East Germany in 1986.
