
PROBLEMS in Australia were largely to blame for the financial crisis which
struck Bank of New Zealand (BNZ) in September 1990, shareholders were told
at a 5 1/2 -hour extraordinary meeting on Friday, writes Terry Hall in
Wellington.
Two years ago BNZ directors disclosed for the second time a surge in bad
debt provisions, and were forced to ask its main shareholders the New
Zealand government and Fay, Richwhite the local merchant bank for help.
