
DELEGATES at Congress reading the Morning Star, the communist newspaper,
which yesterday appealed to union leaders for assistance in what many see as
a last-ditch attempt to avoid closure. It was launched in 1930 as the Daily
Worker and renamed the Morning Star in 1968. Daily sales are down to 7,000 -
about 2,000 short of what is needed for a trading surplus - while losses in
the past two years are about Pounds 500,000. The staff of 40 have not had
full pay for several weeks.
