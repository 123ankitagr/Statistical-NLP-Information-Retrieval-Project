the budget is set to make the leap from battered despatch box to information superhighway scarcely will mr kenneth clarke the chancellor have sat down after delivering his speech on november 29 than the full text of his remarks will be ready to speed round the globe courtesy of the internet symbol of the wired society the internet a semiofficial selfregulating system of networks which connects about 7000 databases and 2m computers is emerging as the de facto information superhighway up to 25m people worldwide will be able to tap into treasury computers examine the chancellors arguments in detail and download paragraphs that interest them most internet users are in the us but more than 1m britons now have the computer modem and software which guarantees them access to the worlds largest computer network the treasury was vague about the number of people who might benefit from access to mr clarkes words at the speed of light but it thought some internet users were academics researchers or accountants with an interest in uk fiscal policy the budget on the internet is one of the first consequences of a treasury plan to use the latest technology to promote more open government the treasury intends to publish news releases ministers speeches minutes of the chancellors monthly meeting with the governor of the bank of england and reports from the panel of independent forecasters the wise men on the internet as a first step towards more comprehensive public exposure the service will be free of charge and available to anybody with internet access the chancellor will not be able to follow suit he has access to the internet neither at home nor at the office the daily telegraph launched an electronic version of its newspaper yesterday the electronic telegraph is available on the internet from 7am every day the internet home page for the treasury world wide web service is httpwww hmtreasury gov uk
