time warner the entertainment and publishing giant and viacom international a cable and broadcast television group yesterday said they had settled an anti-trust lawsuit linked to the 1989 merger between time and warner viacom which had sought dollars 2.4 bn in damages from time said it would receive material financial benefits from the settlement in addition to strategic advantages time warner which has not admitted any liability to viacom's claims will pay viacom an undisclosed cash sum other terms of the settlement include wider distribution of viacom's showtime and movie channels on time warner's cable system joint marketing campaigns by showtime and time warner's hbo and increased advertising purchases by time warner companies on viacom's mtv networks over the next five years in addition time warner will buy viacom's milwaukee cable system in the original suit viacom had alleged that time warner had used its power as the second largest us operator of cable television networks to promote its own pay-tv film services at the expense of viacom's services viacom charged that the uncompetitive acts dating to at least 1980, would worsen after the merger between time and warner
