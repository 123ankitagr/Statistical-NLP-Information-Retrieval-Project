the city council has approved the development of a 122-unit senior citizen apartment complex that had been delayed for months after neighbors objected to increased traffic and noise from the project the complex to be located on a 2.7 acre site on the north side of carlson lane and the east side of bradford avenue was approved unanimously this week after the developers huntington properties of huntington beach agreed to construct an eight-foot fence around much of the site to reduce noise and ensure privacy they also agreed to make sure trash bins are not placed in back of someone's home the city council amended its development standards for the project to allow fewer parking spaces and less open space than what has been required some council members said that it fulfills a demand for more senior citizen housing in the community there's certainly a need for this in this community said councilman john o tynes
