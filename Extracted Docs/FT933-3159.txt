general motors the us carmaker is looking to acquire one or several japanese parts makers to increase sales of components in the japanese market mr philip austin vice-president of gm's automotive components group in japan said the company could acquire several small components makers or a larger company to be a strong player in any market you need a manufacturing presence and we don't have one in japan although acquisitions of japanese companies by foreign groups have been rare mr austin said the timing was good as many japanese car parts suppliers had suffered from the economic downturn their investments in the us have become a burden and they are overcommitted in japan with borrowings so opportunities obviously exist gm's move reflects its growing interest in developing its asian operations the components division which sells more than 70 per cent of its product to the parent company is working to reduce its dependence on the north american market to 50 per cent of revenues by the year 2000 us and japanese negotiators meet in hawaii next week to discuss encouraging japanese purchases of us car and car parts in an effort to reduce japan's huge trade surplus us-made car parts have been more successful in the japanese market than us cars gm sells more than twice as much in components in japan than it does in cars and mr austin said gm expects sales to japanese carmakers to reach dollars 2 bn pounds 1.2 bn globally by 1998, compared with dollars 900 m last year
