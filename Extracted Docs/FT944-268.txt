Standard & Poors , the rating agency , has raised its credit ratings for Thailand and Malaysia 's long-term foreign-currency debt . Malaysia 's rating was raised to single-A-plus from single-A while Thailand 's rating was raised to single-A from single-A-minus , with a stable long-term outlook for both . Malaysia 's upgrading reflects the country 's steady progress in diversifying its export-based economy , prudent macroeconomic management and a political system that balances the demands of its multi-ethnic society , the agency said . Thailand 's upgrading reflects the maintenance of the country's external payments position and the consolidation of civilian-led democratic rule since 1992 .
