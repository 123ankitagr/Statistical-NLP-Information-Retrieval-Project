china is considering opening its nonferrous metals mines to foreign investment and broadening its own investments in copper mines overseas according to the china daily reports reuter from beijing the official newspaper quoted mr liu wanling an official at china national nonferrous metals industry corporation as saying that his country was keen to raise its production of restricted metals like aluminium and copper and would seek overseas capital to enable it do so china has eased shortages of most nonferrous metals with the exception of copper and wants to mobilise foreign investment to exploit overseas copper mines mr liu said officials have inspected mines in australia south america africa and russia we hope to raise foreign funds to construct copper mines in foreign countries mr liu said although he acknowledged that would not be easy as the worlds best mines were already occupied chinas broader reah overseas would coincide with the opening of its nonferrous mines to foreign capital especially for zinc lead and other metals abundant on the mainland he said china had begun negotiations on proposed joint ventures in the nonferrous metals industry that would be far larger than the 220 existing joint ventures mr liu said chinas longterm hope is to raise per capita consumption levels closer to world standards thereby boosting production for example the chinese consume less than 1kg of aluminium per head a year far below the 20kg to 30kg in industrialised countries
