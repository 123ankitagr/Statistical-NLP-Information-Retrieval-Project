A massive earthquake with a magnitude of 8.2 -- the world 's strongest quake in more than two years -- occurred in the South Pacific about 850 miles southwest of New Zealand today . There was no immediate report of damage or injuries and no tsunami waves resulted . New Zealand said the quake was picked up by seismological monitors in the capital of Wellington , but there were no reports that the tremor was felt in New Zealand . A spokesman for the U.S. Geological Survey in Washington said the quake struck the sparsely populated Macquarie Islands region of the South Pacific .
