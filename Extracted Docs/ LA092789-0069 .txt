The Olympic Games will go on cable television for the first time under a $ 50-million deal worked out between CBS Inc. and Turner Broadcasting Systems Inc. that covers the 1992 and 1994 Winter Olympics . Turner Network Television will air about 50 hours of programming for each Olympics . CBS , which paid $ 243 million for the 1992 Games at Albertville , France , and $ 300 million for the 1994 Games at Lillehammer , Norway , will air about 120 hours of each Olympics .
