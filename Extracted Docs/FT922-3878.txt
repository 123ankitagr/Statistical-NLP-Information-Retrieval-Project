TEA The market remained selective reports the Tea Broker 's Association . Better liquoring East Africans attracted good competition with brightest Kenyas 5 to 10p dearer . Mediums , however , were often easier and prices for plain teas also showed a decline . Offshore improved demand in the cif section with brighter descriptions about steady and Zimbabwe offerings a strong feature . Fob teas from Mauritius met limited support . The highest price realised this week was 200p for a Rwanda . Quotations : quality 155p . nom good medium 138p . medium 100p . low medium 83p .
