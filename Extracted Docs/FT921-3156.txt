THE Italian government yesterday invoked special powers to cancel a proposed strike by air traffic controllers called for today , Robert Graham reports from Rome . Similar powers are likely to be invoked for another stoppage planned by different air traffic control unions for tomorrow . Mr Carlo Bernini , transport minister , ordered air traffic controllers to report to work as normal ; those defying the order could face up to five years ' jail . Strikes last Friday and Saturday by the air traffic controllers forced cancellation of over 80 per cent of all flights in and out of Italy . The stoppages were called as part of a dispute over work conditions .
