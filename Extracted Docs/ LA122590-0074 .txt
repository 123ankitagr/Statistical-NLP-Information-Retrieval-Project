African National Congress deputy leader Nelson Mandela , celebrating his first Christmas at home in nearly 30 years , appealed to South Africans on Monday to work for peace so blacks never again spend the holiday `` in chains . '' In Pretoria , ANC President Oliver Tambo met for the first time with President F. W. de Klerk . The meeting was described as a `` courtesy visit . '' Afterward , Tambo told reporters he hopes South Africa will have a `` full recovery from apartheid '' by Christmas next year . Mandela , ANC deputy president , read a 2 1/2-page message in which he lamented political violence but expressed optimism for peace . `` Let us vow never to celebrate another Christmas in chains , '' he said . In related news , police said eight people died during the weekend in unrest in Natal province , including two people shot to death by police .
