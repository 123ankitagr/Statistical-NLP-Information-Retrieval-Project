ansaldo volund a danish subsidiary of ansaldo the italian state-controlled engineering company and chung-hsin electric machinery its taiwanese partner have won contracts worth dollars 300 m pounds 205.4 m to build waste-to-energy incinerators for two taiwanese cities andrew hill writes from milan the orders are worth about dollars 200 m to chung-hsin and dollars 100 m to ansaldo volund for a four-year project which will generate electricity for the equivalent of 20,000 families in the cities of pali and hsinchu ansaldo which is controlled by the italian state engineering group finmeccanica said the award would give ansaldo volund 40 per cent of the taiwanese market for incinerating urban waste or 50 per cent if the share of nkk the japanese company to which the danish group has granted a licence is included the group has won orders for two other incinerators in taiwan
