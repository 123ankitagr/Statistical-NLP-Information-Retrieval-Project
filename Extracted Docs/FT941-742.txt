environment secretary mr john gummer yesterday opened the five-mile extension to the docklands light railway to beckton in east london after a delay of 18 months the pounds 280 m project to extend the railway has been dogged by problems with construction and systems installation the extension which connects beckton with the isle of dogs and the city is funded by the london docklands development corporation set up to regenerate the old docks area the extension takes the dlr to within a few hundred yards of the city airport in east london but in a hangover from the poor transport planning which has bedevilled development of the area there is still no station at the airport mr gummer also launched the west silvertown urban village a development of 1,500 homes by peabody trust the charitable housing provider together with the lddc the local authority and tate lyle the sugar and sweeteners group the scheme based on the prince of wales ideas of integrated communities is being backed by the government's private finance initiative and will be built over eight to 10 years
