avid bundesbank watchers will be keeping a close eye on this weeks meeting of the german central banks council at which the money supply target for 1995 will be set to see what clues emerge about monetary policy the bundesbank has made no interest rate changes since may and economists are divided as to when rates will start heading upwards again this year m3 has soared alarmingly before recently falling back to within striking distance of the target range of 4 per cent to 6 per cent the 1995 target is expected to be about the same for the moment the bundesbank is keeping the markets calm knowing how volatile they have been as bond market rates have risen in the wake of us antiinflationary moves next year is likely to be a lot calmer for the bond market than 1994 has been the 10year bund yield started the year at 555 per cent its lowest since 1986 and reached a high of 78 per cent in october from which it has eased slightly commerzbank expects the 1995 level to average 68 per cent with a similar level for 1996 the favourable inflation trend and the easing in m3 provide a stable basis for the bond market the bank argues with domestic investors expected to become more enthusiastic
