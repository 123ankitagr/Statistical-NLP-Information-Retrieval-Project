the us and north korea have agreed on speedy resumption of talks between pyongyang and the international atomic energy agency designed to pave the way for international inspections of two suspect north korean nuclear sites but the us failed to secure a pledge that north korea would allow iaea inspections of the sites at the yongbyon nuclear complex mr robert gallucci assistant us secretary of state admitted there was more work to do but said yesterday's agreement was a step in the right direction the agreement came at a second round of bilateral discussions in geneva a third round of talks is planned within the next two months north korea will continue to suspend its decision in march to withdraw from the 1968 nuclear non-proliferation treaty which forbids non-nuclear states from acquiring nuclear weapons and requires iaea inspections to guard against diversion of nuclear materials for weapons use in an agreed statement the two sides said north korea was prepared to begin consultations with the iaea on outstanding safeguards and other issues as soon as possible mr kang sok chu north korea's first deputy foreign minister said these consultations would include pyongyang's insistence on iaea impartiality north korea has cited iaea bias as grounds for refusing access to the two buildings where the agency believes plutonium may be stored the statement reaffirms in general terms us pledges not to use force against a peaceful north korea but pyongyang did not secure the assurance it apparently wanted that next year's joint us south korean team spirit military exercises would be dropped the statement also calls for talks between north and south korea as soon as possible on the nuclear and other bilateral issues mr kang said north korea was proposing a high-level meeting to pave the way for a summit in a surprise development the us has agreed in principle to help north korea convert its nuclear power stations from graphite moderated reactors to light-water moderated reactors mr gallucci said this was conditional on north korea being in full compliance with the npt regime including inspections mr gallucci said light water reactors were more proliferation-resistant' than provocative graphite moderated reactors because they required importation of enriched uranium and thus made countries dependent on the political goodwill of suppliers
