the medical research council mrc and wellcome trust are joining forces to set up a genetic research centre in cambridge the centre which will have a budget of several million pounds a year will extend a research project led by dr john sulston of the mrc laboratory of molecular biology in cambridge early this year dr sulston was considering an offer to join a genetics company in the us a prospect that dismayed british genetic researchers but the prospect of new funding has persuaded him to stay in the uk dr sulston is working out the genome the entire genetic blueprint of the nematode worm c elegans which provides a simplified model of the human genome the centre will continue the worm genome project and begin to apply its lessons to the much larger task of finding the sequence of chemical letters in the 100,000 human genes several sites in cambridge are being considered for it the research will have strong international links especially with the us france and japan the wellcome trust is the world's largest medical charity with a budget of more than pounds 100 m a year half as much as the state-funded mrc derived mainly from its majority stake in the wellcome drug company
