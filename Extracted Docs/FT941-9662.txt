scientists yesterday reported significant promise in the century-long struggle to develop a vaccine against malaria reuter reports malaria kills up to 1 m children a year in africa and there are up to 500 m cases of the disease each year globally the disease is showing resistance to drugs traditionally used to treat it scientists working for a tropical disease programme jointly sponsored by the un development programme world bank and world health organisation said a vaccine known as spf66 has shown promising results in first phases of human trials in a region of africa
