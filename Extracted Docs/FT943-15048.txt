commercial and residential properties in london's docklands are letting and selling faster than at any stage since the late 1980 s the london docklands development corporation said yesterday some 800,000 sq ft of offices were let during the 12 months to the end of march the largest annual total since 1987 and 50 per cent more than the previous two years combined mr michael pickard corporation chairman said construction of 1,000 homes also is under way in the area after a sharp rise in private-sector sales new residential development had virtually ground to a halt during the previous few years mr pickard announcing the publication of the corporation's annual report yesterday said the tide has definitely turned and is now running firmly in london docklands favour almost 500,000 sq ft of offices had been let since january more than half of the office space in canary wharf which came out of administrative receivership last year was let about 5,600 jobs were created last year taking the total employed in the 8.5 sq miles of docklands to 55,600 this compares with 27,200 when the corporation was formed in 1981 several factors had encouraged more tenants to choose the area these included the opening of the limehouse link which had radically improved road access to the city the opening of the docklands light railway extension to beckton and the start of work on the jubilee underground line extension transport to and within the area was now as good as anywhere in london mr pickard said the reliability rate of the docklands light railway was 98 per cent last year nonetheless there was still much to be done he said docklands had more than 5 m sq ft of empty offices representing a vacancy rate of more than 40 per cent since the corporation was formed in 1981 it has acquired 1,146 acres of land for development of this 738 acres have been sold to developers leaving 408 acres to be developed the corporation is due gradually to hand over its responsibilities to local authorities prior to being wound up in march 1998
