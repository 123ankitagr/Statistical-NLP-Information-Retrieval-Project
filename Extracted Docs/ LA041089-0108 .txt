A short-circuit apparently caused a fire that led to the deaths of 42 crew members of a Soviet nuclear-powered submarine off the Norwegian coast , Izvestia said today . The government newspaper also revealed that four of the victims died aboard the submarine when it caught fire and sank Friday . The rest apparently drowned . Twenty-nine were rescued from Arctic waters , but two died later . `` According to provisional data , the fire started as a result of an electrical short-circuit , '' Sergei Vargin , head of the political department of the Soviet Northern Fleet , told Izvestia . `` It started when the submarine was returning from a distant voyage and was sailing under water . When the accident happened , the submarine surfaced . '' No radioactive contamination occurred , Soviet Defense Minister Dmitry Yazov said .
