THE International Air Transport Association ( Iata ) said yesterday worldwide traffic showed a healthy increase in January in what it described as a hopeful sign for the recession-hit industry , Reuter reports from Geneva . According to an Iata statement , international passenger traffic rose by 14 per cent over January last year and freight traffic rose by 10 per cent . 'Worldwide double-digit growth for both passenger and freight traffic in January is a hopeful sign for the airlines , ' said Mr Gunter Eser , the Iata director general . But he added , 'We have to remember that traffic in January 1991 was depressed by the Gulf War . '
