MR LOUIS FREEH was yesterday sworn in as the fifth director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation , Reuter reports from Washington . Mr Freeh , 43 , a former FBI agent who later served as a federal prosecutor and judge in New York City , was nominated by President Bill Clinton in July to replace Mr William Sessions , who had been appointed by former President George Bush . Mr Sessions was fired by Mr Clinton for weak leadership and abusing the privileges of his office . Mr Clinton said he hoped the FBI in the 1990s would become best known for cracking down on drug lords and terrorists .
