october care tips de-thatch or aerate your lawn to prevent disease and pests during the winter prepare rhododendrons and azaleas for winter by mulching heavily around their base with 2 to 3 inches of mulch when planting bulbs be generous with the bone meal place some at the bottom of the planting hole add a little dirt and then the bulb so the bulb doesn't touch the bone meal if you're thinking of planting ground cover plant it soon if you do it now the root system will become established before spring arrives and growth accelerates thin camellia buds now for bigger and better blooms later for beautiful red and orange color in the fall plant a pomegranate tree or shrub to give your yard a fresh new look uproot plants that have overgrown their space tend to your roses by removing dead wood
