SOUTH KOREA 'S biggest steelmaker , Pohang Iron and Steel , and its former chairman Mr Park Tae-joon evaded nearly 80bn won ( Pounds 64m ) in tax in 1990 , South Korea 's tax office alleged yesterday , Reuter reports from Seoul . The National Tax Administration ( NTA ) said that , of the unpaid amount , 73bn won belonged to the company and its subsidiaries , and 6.3bn won to Mr Park . An NTA official said the tax office had filed suit against Mr Park , a former co-chairman of the ruling Democratic Liberal Party , alleging diversion of company funds for personal purposes .
