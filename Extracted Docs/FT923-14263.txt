the us food and drug administration yesterday asked marion merrell dow the american pharmaceuticals group to warn doctors that seldane its anti-histamine is potentially dangerous to certain patients the drug had worldwide sales of dollars 768 m last year of which dollars 534 m were in the us in the uk the company claims seldane is the best-selling over-the-counter remedy the fda said the drug was potentially dangerous when used by patients with damaged liver or those taking two other drugs ketoconazole an anti-fungal treatment and erythromycin an antibiotic although one patient has died the fda stressed reactions were rare marion merrell dow said it was changing the labels warning of such problems and the need to restrict dosing to less than 120 mg a day
