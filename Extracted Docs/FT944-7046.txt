Despite Generali increasing its annuity rates by 1.5 % , the downward trend has continued , writes Peter Quinton of the Annuity Bureau . Canada Life moved its annuity rates down by 0.8 % on November 21 , and , on November 22 , three other life companies , London Life , Prudential and Abbey Life chose to reduce their rates by 1.1 % , 1 % and 0.08 % , respectively . The downward trend is in part due to actuaries responding to a reduction in gilt yields . An annuity provides a guaranteed income for life in return for a lump sum investment . The bulk of the fund built up by many types of pension plan must be used in this way .
