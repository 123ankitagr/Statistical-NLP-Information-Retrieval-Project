Average earnings rose an underlying 3 per cent in the year to October , unchanged from September and still the slowest rate of growth for 26 years , Emma Tucker writes . Earnings growth in manufacturing held steady at 4.25 per cent , while the government-imposed 1.5 per cent limit on public-sector pay increases helped keep service-sector earnings growth down to 2.25 per cent . Both rates were unchanged from the previous month , but the September figures for services and manufacturing were revised down by 0.25 per cent . The rate of decline for unit wage costs slowed in October , reflecting rises in employment .
