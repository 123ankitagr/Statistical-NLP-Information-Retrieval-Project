Australian Mutual Provident Society ( AMP ) , Australia 's biggest insurance and investment institution , plans to tap the emerging consumer demand for insurance in Asia by investing in an Indonesian life insurance concern , AP-DJ reports from Sydney . AMP said it would enter into a transaction to invest more than ADollars 100m ( Dollars 65m ) to buy 40 per cent of Panin Life Insurance , the largest life insurance operation in terms of assets in Indonesia , and establish a joint venture with Panin Life . Panin Life is owned by Panin Bank , one of the largest privately held banks in the country .
