a long and costly effort by the the australian federal government to extradite mr christopher skase one of australia's high-flying entrepreneurs in the 1980 s and head of the now-collapsed qintex group appeared to have collapsed yesterday when a spanish court upheld his appeal and freed him the full bench of the audiencia nacional spain's highest court made its decision known only indirectly and it was several hours before australian officials learned of it the court's reasons are not likely to be known before monday australia had sought his extradition so he could face 32 charges arising from the collapse of his media and resort empire in 1989 the failure left corporate debts of around adollars 1.5 bn pounds 680 m and additional personal debts
