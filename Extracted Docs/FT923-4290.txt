the river tees between stockton and middlesbrough was diverted yesterday as part of the construction work on the pounds 47 m tees barrage chris tighe writes an earthbank plug edging the river was removed by excavator allowing the water to gush into a newly built 400 metre navigation channel through which it will flow until the barrage is completed in two years next week an upstream plug will be also be removed completing the temporary diversion of the river at a point where it is more than 60 metres wide the changed course of the river will allow tarmac the contractors for the barrage to drain the riverbed and build the barrage in the dry mr john franklin tarmac project manager said it's very unusual to divert a river of this magnitude normally you don't have enough room beside a river to divert it the diverting of the tees was financially viable he added because a navigation channel is part of the scheme the project funded by the teesside development corporation will raise the level of the tees at stockton covering mudflats creating leisure facilities and providing a new road crossing it will also feed canals cut through the adjacent teesdale site scene of mrs thatcher's celebrated walk in the wilderness in 1987 and now targeted by the tdc for pounds 500 m public and private investment
