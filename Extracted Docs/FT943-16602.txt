representatives of more than 30 leading brand manufacturers will tomorrow formalise a campaign group to press for legislation curbing supermarkets lookalike brands neil buckley writes the british producers and brand owners group set up earlier this year failed to get clauses included in the trademarks bill to strengthen restrictions on lookalikes ownlabel products designed to resemble wellknown brands the main aim of the group which includes unilever mars and nestle will be to persuade the government to introduce legislation it may also investigate the possibility of taking action against supermarkets under european union law ownlabel products account for more than 50 per cent of sales by volume in supermarket chains brand manufacturers want legislation to stop supermarkets not only infringing registered trademarks but copying the overall look of branded goods including the colour and shape of packaging
