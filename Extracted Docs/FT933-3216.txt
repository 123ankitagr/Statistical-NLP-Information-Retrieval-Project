speed limits are to be cut to 20 mph on more residential roads after pilot schemes led to a sharp fall in accidents the government said yesterday mr robert key the roads minister said at the launch of a road safety campaign in london that there was evidence slowing down saved lives officials monitoring 60 experimental schemes involving road calming and 20 mph zones found child deaths had fallen by 80 per cent in accidents where pedestrians and cyclists were hit by cars the overall number of casualties in traffic accidents was down 70 per cent statistics compiled by the department of transport's traffic research laboratory show last year 44,186 children were involved in road accidents however and britain's child pedestrian death rate is still one of the highest in europe
