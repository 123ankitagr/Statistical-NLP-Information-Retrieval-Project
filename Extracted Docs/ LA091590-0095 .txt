Thirteen people protesting U.S. policy in El Salvador were arrested after a mass protest that stopped traffic and created a blocks-long jam in West Los Angeles Friday afternoon . The 3 p.m. protest , outside the office of U.S. Sen. Pete Wilson ( R-Calif. ) at Santa Monica and Sepulveda boulevards , drew more than 100 protesters who demanded that Wilson oppose aid to El Salvador , said an organizer , Neile Rissmiller . Eight men and five women were arrested and released on $ 250 bail , police said . The demonstration , organized by the Committee in Solidarity with the People of El Salvador , shut down the eastbound lanes of Santa Monica Boulevard and tied up traffic on nearby freeway ramps .
