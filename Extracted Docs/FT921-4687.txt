PERSONAL computer ( PC ) users reported scattered outbreaks of the Michelangelo computer virus yesterday , but the widespread damage predicted by computer security companies did not materialise , write Louise Kehoe and Paul Taylor . The virus , named after the Renaissance master born 517 years ago yesterday , caused sporadic damage across the globe to IBM-compatible PCs . In the UK at least eight companies were struck and software programs were wiped out on more than 100 computers . Scotland Yard police said : 'The impact has been more severe than we thought . '
