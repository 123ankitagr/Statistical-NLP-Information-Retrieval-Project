a steady fall in export prices for commodities is punishing people who are already on the margins of a decent human existence according to rev charles elliott one of the uks leading development economists and former head of christian aid writes david dodwell world trade editor in a speech to be given in london last night marking the 50th anniversary of oxfam the uk aid organisation mr elliott argued that the international commodity price system has been a powerful process whereby wealth and income have been transferred from commodity producers to commodity importersmanufacturing exporters he said the import purchasing power of developing countries had fallen by 2 per cent a year since 1950 as a result of this process
