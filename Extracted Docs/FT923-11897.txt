the swedish export council has warned companies that they should export to the former soviet union only on an advancepayment basis after a survey showed unpaid debt to swedish firms had nearly doubled in six months reuter reports from stockholm in its survey covering 32 swedish companies the export council said payments due from companies in the former soviet union mainly russia grew to skr456m pounds 44m in june from skr256m in january our general recommendation is that swedish companies should demand advance payments or bank guarantees before delivering any goods mr staffan stenstrom at the export council said swedish exports to the former soviet union totalled skr23bn in 1991
