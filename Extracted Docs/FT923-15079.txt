changes to the pension age would not affect anyone expecting to retire in the next few years mr peter lilley social security secretary emphasised yesterday alison smith writes with the consultation period on the equalisation of the state pension age just ended mr lilley said that no decisions had yet been taken and that all the 3000plus responses would be fully considered the expectation has been that a flexible decade of retirement has in effect been ruled out and that the costs of lowering the retirement age for men to 60 would be too great leaving the raising of the retirement age for women as the most likely option
